Signs Your Hard Drive May Be Failing

If a drive is failing, the best thing you can do is stop heavy use and back up what you can. Continued use can reduce recovery chances.

If you hear clicking (HDD) or the PC freezes during file access, treat it seriously.

Warning signs

  • Very slow loading and file access
  • Frequent freezes or crashes
  • Disk errors / SMART warnings
  • Clicking noises (HDD)

What to do first

  1. Back up important files.
  2. Stop heavy use.
  3. Schedule a clone/replacement.

How Subtronics helps

  • Clone failing drives to SSD
  • Replace and migrate Windows
  • Recovery options if cloning isn’t possible

FAQ

Should I keep using a failing drive?

No. Back up and reduce usage—continued use can reduce recovery chances.

Can you clone a failing drive?

Often yes, depending on severity. The sooner you stop heavy use, the better.
