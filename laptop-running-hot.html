Laptop Running Hot? Try This First

Laptops run hotter than desktops, but they shouldn’t be burning hot, constantly throttling, or shutting down.

Most laptop overheating is dust buildup or worn thermal paste.

Common causes

  • Dust clogged heatsink/fan
  • Worn thermal paste
  • Blocked vents
  • Failing fan

Safe steps to try

  1. Use a hard surface and keep vents clear.
  2. Check Task Manager for high CPU usage.
  3. Switch to Balanced power mode temporarily.
  4. Clean external vents gently.

When it needs service

  • Overheats quickly even on a desk
  • Fan rattles or doesn’t spin
  • Shuts down under load

FAQ

Can overheating cause shutdowns?

Yes. Many laptops shut down automatically to protect components.

Why is my laptop hot while idle?

Background processes, dust buildup, or a failing fan can cause heat at idle.
