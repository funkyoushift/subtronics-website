Gaming PC Pricing: Prebuilt and Custom Options

This is the main human-facing buying page for both Tier 1 prebuilt gaming PCs and custom gaming PC builds. Compare both options here without bouncing between extra pages.

Important buying note:
Sometimes the better move is to buy a strong value prebuilt and have it professionally set up, cleaned up, updated, and optimized instead of stretching a custom build into the wrong budget.

How to use this page

  1. Start with Tier 1 prebuilt pricing if your budget is tight or you want the fastest path.
  2. Move into starter or higher custom tiers if part quality, airflow, noise, and upgrade room matter more.

When a prebuilt is the smarter buy

  • You want the most gaming performance for the money.
  • You want to stay under about $1,200 to $1,400.
  • You want the fastest path to getting up and running.
  • You would rather pay for setup and optimization than premium custom parts.

A good prebuilt plus local optimization is often the best-value route for budget-conscious buyers.

Ask about a prebuilt

When custom is worth it

  • You want better part quality from day one.
  • You care about thermals, airflow, noise, and upgrade path.
  • You want a cleaner, more tailored parts list.
  • You are shopping above basic budget territory.

Custom starts to make more sense when you want more control over parts quality and long-term headroom.
