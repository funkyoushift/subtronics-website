Computer Randomly Restarts – Troubleshooting Guide

Random restarts can come from several different causes, which is why they frustrate people so much. Instead of throwing parts at the problem, use a process that separates heat, power, memory, and Windows-related issues.

Basic steps

  1. Notice when the restarts happen: during games, during startup, at idle, or at random with no pattern.
  2. Check for overheating symptoms like loud fans, very high temperatures, or shutdowns under load.
  3. Reseat RAM if it was recently installed and remove any unstable overclock or XMP/EXPO profile for testing.
  4. Inspect power cables and consider the age and quality of the power supply if the system is older.
  5. Review Windows update history, driver changes, and event logs for clues around the restart time.
  6. Test one variable at a time so you do not lose track of what actually changed the behavior.

Common mistakes to avoid

  • Replacing multiple parts at once makes it harder to know what solved the issue.
  • A weak power supply can restart a PC without leaving obvious visual damage.
  • Heat and memory instability often show up only under heavier workloads.

When DIY stops being worth it

There is nothing wrong with doing the safe part yourself and then handing the rest off once the job becomes stressful, time-consuming, or risky. That is especially true when the computer has important files, expensive parts, or symptoms that do not match a simple upgrade.
