How to Upgrade RAM in a Desktop PC

A RAM upgrade is one of the easiest ways to improve multitasking, browser responsiveness, and general desktop performance when your system is otherwise in good shape.

This Paul’s Hardware tutorial is a strong walkthrough for physically installing RAM and avoiding common slot-order mistakes.

Step-by-step basics

  1. Look up your motherboard or prebuilt model to confirm the correct memory type, speed range, and maximum capacity.
  2. Shut the PC down fully, switch off the power supply, and unplug the computer.
  3. Hold the power button for a few seconds to discharge leftover power.
  4. Open the side panel and locate the RAM slots next to the CPU cooler.
  5. If you are replacing existing memory, unlock the retaining clips and remove the old sticks carefully.
  6. Line up the notch on the new RAM with the slot, then press evenly until both clips click into place.
  7. Use the recommended slots from the motherboard manual when installing two sticks, usually alternating slots for dual-channel mode.
  8. Boot into BIOS or Windows and verify that the full amount of memory is detected.

Things to avoid

  • Do not force RAM into a slot. If the notch does not line up, it is the wrong module or wrong orientation.
  • Mixing mismatched kits can work, but it can also create stability issues or force slower settings.
  • If the PC shows a black screen after the upgrade, power down and reseat the memory one stick at a time.

When to hand it off

DIY is great when the job is safe, simple, and you have time to work slowly. Once the job turns into a data-risk situation, repeated boot failures, stripped screws, BIOS confusion, or expensive-part anxiety, it usually makes sense to stop and get help.
