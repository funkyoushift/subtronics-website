How to Apply Thermal Paste to a CPU

Thermal paste helps transfer heat from the CPU into the cooler. Applying it is simple, but sloppy prep or the wrong pressure can create a mess and lead to poor temperatures.

This JayzTwoCents tutorial is a quick, easy primer on applying paste without overthinking it.

Step-by-step basics

  1. If you are redoing an old cooler, remove the old paste with isopropyl alcohol and a lint-free cloth until both surfaces are clean.
  2. Make sure the CPU heat spreader and cooler base are dry before applying anything new.
  3. Place a small amount of thermal paste in the center of the CPU unless your cooler or paste brand recommends a different pattern.
  4. Mount the cooler evenly and tighten it in an alternating pattern so pressure spreads the paste properly.
  5. Plug the CPU fan or pump into the correct header before powering on.
  6. After booting, monitor temperatures to make sure the cooler is seated correctly.

Things to avoid

  • Too little or too much paste is usually less damaging than a poorly seated cooler.
  • Do not spread paste with dirty fingers or reuse dried-out old paste.
  • If temperatures spike instantly after boot, shut down and re-check cooler mounting pressure.

When to hand it off

DIY is great when the job is safe, simple, and you have time to work slowly. Once the job turns into a data-risk situation, repeated boot failures, stripped screws, BIOS confusion, or expensive-part anxiety, it usually makes sense to stop and get help.
