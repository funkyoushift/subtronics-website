Computer Won’t Turn On? Start Here

When a PC won’t turn on, it’s usually power-related — but it can also be a display issue that looks like “dead computer.”

Use the checklist below. If you’re ever unsure, stop and call/text us.

Before anything else

  • Desktop: Confirm the monitor is on and connected. Try a different cable if you have one.
  • Laptop: Look for a charging light. No light can mean charger or charging port problems.

Safe troubleshooting checklist

  1. Try a different outlet (no power strip).
  2. Confirm power cable is fully seated (PC + wall).
  3. Do a hard reset: hold power 10–15s, unplug 60s, then retry.
  4. Unplug all USB devices and try again.
  5. If lights come on but no display: try a different monitor/cable.

When a technician is needed

  • No lights/fans even after outlet + hard reset
  • Burning smell, popping sounds, or visible damage
  • Turns on then immediately shuts off
  • Repeated clicking (possible drive issues)

FAQ

Why won’t my computer turn on but the keyboard lights up?

Power may be present, but the system may be failing to start due to RAM/GPU/motherboard/display issues.

Is a power supply failure common?

Yes. Desktop power supplies can fail over time or after surges. Testing is quick.
