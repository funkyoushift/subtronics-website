How to Fix a Slow Computer

If your computer suddenly feels sluggish, the cause is usually one of a few common issues: storage nearly full, too many startup programs, malware/adware, overheating, or an old hard drive.

Work through the steps below in order. Stop if you’re not comfortable — you can always call us and we’ll handle it.

Quick checks (2 minutes)

  • Is it only one program? If Windows feels normal but one app is slow, focus on that app.
  • Is the drive almost full? Low space can slow updates and performance.
  • Is the PC hot or loud? That often means throttling from heat.

Step-by-step troubleshooting

  1. Restart and test again.
  2. Check free storage (Settings → System → Storage).
  3. Disable startup apps (Task Manager → Startup).
  4. Run a full malware scan (Windows Security).
  5. Check for overheating (hot chassis, loud fans, shutdowns).
  6. Check drive type (HDD vs SSD). SSD upgrade is a common fix.

When it’s time for diagnostics

  • Slow even after cleanup
  • Freezing, random crashes, or constant “Not Responding”
  • Drive clicking or repeated disk errors
  • Overheating/shutdowns

At that point, a quick diagnostic can save you from buying the wrong parts.

FAQ

Why is my computer slow after updates?

Updates can temporarily use CPU/disk. If it stays slow, check startup apps, storage, and drive type.

What’s the biggest upgrade for speed?

On older PCs: upgrading from HDD to SSD is usually the most noticeable improvement.
